Experiment 2: Linear probe for pathogen detection

Purpose
This experiment tests whether pathogen status is linearly recoverable from sequence-level SAE features. If a simple logistic regression performs well, then the SAE representation contains accessible pathogen/non-pathogen information.

Inputs
- features.npy: a sequence-by-latent SAE activation matrix, expected to be 20,000 sequences by 32,768 latents.
- sequence_ids.json: row order for features.npy.
- data/human_virus_class1_labeled.jsonl: labels for each sequence, where source=1 means pathogen and source=0 means non-pathogen.

What it does
1. Loads SAE feature activations and aligns each feature row to its pathogen label using sequence_ids.json.
2. Splits the data into stratified train and test sets with an 80/20 split.
3. Trains an L2-regularized logistic regression with cross-validation over regularization strength.
4. Evaluates test accuracy, Matthews correlation coefficient, AUROC, and the classification report.
5. Reads the learned coefficient for each SAE latent.
6. Ranks the most positive coefficients as pathogen-associated and the most negative coefficients as non-pathogen-associated.
7. For the top 10 latents in each direction, computes:
   - activation frequency in pathogen sequences
   - activation frequency in non-pathogen sequences
   - mean activation in pathogen sequences
   - mean activation in non-pathogen sequences
   - enrichment based on activation frequency
8. Generates plots for coefficient distribution, ROC performance, and mean activation of the top latents by class.

Outputs
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/summary.json: accuracy, MCC, AUROC, best regularization value, train/test counts, and feature count.
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/top_latents.json: top pathogen-associated and non-pathogen-associated latents with coefficients and per-class activation stats.
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/coefficient_distribution.png: histogram of all latent coefficients.
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/roc_curve.png: ROC curve for pathogen classification.
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/top_latent_activations.png: grouped bar chart of mean activation for the top latents by class.
- results/linear_probe_pathogen/api_results.json: frontend/backend payload containing summary, ROC points, coefficient histogram bins, and top latent rows.

How to interpret it
High accuracy, MCC, and AUROC mean pathogen status is strongly encoded in the SAE feature space. Positive coefficients push the probe toward the pathogen class; negative coefficients push it toward the non-pathogen class. Activation frequency measures how often a latent fires. Mean activation measures activation magnitude, averaged over all sequences in a class, including zeros.
